About My Journey.

A bit of background

Jumping into my UX/UI Design career has been a long time coming for me. Since graduation from college, I worked in sales positions as a leasing agent and IT recruiter. Although I enjoyed the challenge of sales and working with people, the most exciting aspect was designing business and resident discount cards.

Just as when I was in college designing posters and clothing as publicity chair for my acapella group, I would design for hours without noticing how much time has passed. Outside of design, I had not yet experienced the phenomenon of being so focused and passionate about a project that it no longer felt like work.

I had previously considered a career in design before but was intimidated to start without any prior HTML or CSS knowledge. When I finally decided to change careers and take Bloc’s web design course, I was still concerned because change involves risk. After creating my first projects, however, I am glad I took this leap and am confident that I made the right decision.



My design philosophy

Because of my experience in customer service as a leasing agent and IT recruiter, along with my Bachelor's degree in Psychology and Sociology, I have the ability to put myself in the mind of our users. I attack design problems with full focus on the user to understand what they want and need from my designs, even if our users don’t know how to articulate it.

I enjoy the design process, especially tweaking and changing elements until my client is pleased. Being comfortable with HTML, CSS, and Javascript allows for seamless communication with the development team so that designer, developer, and client are all on the same page.

Design is a life long learning process, and I am excited to finally embark on a career that I am both proud of and passionate about. To me, web design used to be intimidating-- something completely unattainable. However, after creating my first projects, I am pleased to be prepared for a career in a field that is a match for my interests and talents.
