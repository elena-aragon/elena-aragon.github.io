About Me

I grew up thinking that a career is not supposed to be fun, and was prepared for such a lifestyle. However, in college I discovered one area that did not feel like work-- Designing as Publicity Chair for my acapella group.

I would sit down and space out for hours, forgetting to eat, meticulously changing things until it was perfect. I was spending hours out of my day designing (when I probably should have been studying… ) and would finish feeling proud and exhillterated.

However, after I graduated and went into years of sales, I realized there was something missing. Never once was I so passionate about what I was doing that I forgot the clock.

I had considered working in design before, but looking at professional ads, I was convinced that those designers must have prodigious skill that I didn’t have.

Then, when I was working as an IT recruiter, I interviewed a web designer named Carla and was blown away by her portfolio. We talked more casually about her earlier work, and she thought it would make me laugh to see her first projects. She was right. They were pretty terrible, with frightening color scemes (seriously, one was teal, brick red, and mud brown), horrifying spacing, and comically cliche logos.

I came to the realization that would change my life: People in design are not off the charts creative prodigies. They are totally normal people who took the time to learn design and can now create incredible work. They worked at it and practiced to become the designers they are today. So… What am I waiting for?

To me, web design used to be intimidating-- Something completely out of reach for me. However, after creating my first projects, I am so excited that I took this leap.

For one thing, my initial designs were not horrifying-- They were actually pretty good. It’s fun for me to anticipate a user’s needs and figure out the most creative & attractive solutions possible. For another, I’ve lost a bit of weight being so focused that I forget to eat. And finally, I am actually happy. I enjoy what I’m doing for the first time and I’m proud of myself for making a change.
